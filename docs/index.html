1 芪附汤干预慢性心力衰竭的纯计算研究方案

1.1 Qifu Decoction against Chronic Heart Failure: A Comprehensive In Silico Protocol

2 Overview

2.1 Background

Chronic heart failure (CHF) involves a multidimensional pathological network encompassing neuroendocrine activation, ventricular remodelling, energy metabolic dysregulation, and immuno‑inflammatory responses; hence, single‑target interventions are rarely sufficient to achieve comprehensive therapeutic efficacy.

Qifu decoction (QFD), a famous prescription recorded in Wei Shi Jia Cang Fang, consists of Astragali Radix (Huangqi in Chinese, the dried root of Astragalus membranaceus (Fisch.) Bge.) and Aconiti Lateralis Radix Praeparaia (Fuzi in Chinese, the lateral root of Aconitum carmichaelii Debeaux) with a mass ratio of 2:1.

In clinical practice, this herbal pair is used under the principle of “supplementing qi and warming yang” and has demonstrated consistent efficacy against heart failure. However, the multicomponent, multi‑target, and multi‑organ synergistic mechanisms underlying the Huangqi‑Fuzi herb pair remain inadequately characterised. This study aims to integrate network pharmacology, multi‑organ expression profiling, and molecular docking to quantitatively dissect the molecular basis of the synergistic action of this herb pair in CHF.

Evaluating the pharmacological mechanism of traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) is difficult because the active components and their possible synergistic actions are unclear. [1,2]

2.2 Key Scientific Questions

  • How do the orally bioavailable and blood‑borne active constituents of Qifu Decoction cooperatively interfere with CHF via a multi‑target network?
  • What are the respective contributions of Huangqi (qi‑supplementing) and Fuzi (yang‑warming) at the levels of targets, pathways, and organ distribution, and what quantitative synergistic patterns emerge?
  • Do the core pharmacological targets form a spatially coordinated regulatory network across multiple organs, such as heart, kidney, liver, and vasculature?
  • Can the binding modes and affinities of key active compounds to hub targets provide structural evidence to support the predicted pharmacological effects?

2.3 Objectives

  • To establish the pharmacologically active substance basis and target spectrum of Qifu Decoction based on blood‑absorbed constituents.
  • To identify core hub genes and assign their contributions to Huangqi or Fuzi.
  • To construct a four‑dimensional “component–target–pathway–organ” network and quantitatively evaluate the synergistic effects.
  • To validate core component–target interactions through molecular docking.
  • To provide a clear set of candidate molecules and a theoretical framework for subsequent experimental validation.
